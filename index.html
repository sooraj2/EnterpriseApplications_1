Do it for mothers

An overwhelming 99% of the 500,000 women who die each year during pregnancy and childbirth live in developing countries, with haemorrhage—which invariably requires blood transfusion—the most common cause of maternal deaths.

Do it for children

Many people die because safe blood is not available. In the southern African region, up to 15% of child deaths are attributable to severe anaemia due to malaria. The management of these cases often requires safe and timely blood transfusion.
